THE EXAMPLES

See Swiss Punk influence across decades and disciplines—from 1970s posters to modern digital design.

01

POSTERS & PRINT

The poster was the original canvas for Swiss Punk experimentation. Weingart and his students created iconic works that challenged every rule of traditional typography.

TYPO

Typographic Process

Wolfgang Weingart, 1974

Layered letterforms and halftone dots create depth and movement in this iconic poster.

BASEL

Basel School Exhibition

Basel School of Design, 1978

Bold diagonal compositions and clashing colors announce student exhibitions.

NEW

New Wave Typography

April Greiman, 1982

American interpretation brings California color and early digital elements.

02

ALBUM COVERS

The music industry embraced Swiss Punk's rebellious energy. Post-punk, new wave, and electronic artists found the perfect visual language for their sound.

PUNK

Post-Punk Records

Factory Records, 1979-1985

Peter Saville's designs for Joy Division and New Order merged Swiss Punk with minimalism.

WAVE

New Wave Singles

Various Artists, 1980s

Striped patterns and bold geometry defined the visual identity of synthesizer music.

ELECTRO

Electronic Music

Various Labels, 1990s-Present

The aesthetic continues in techno and electronic music artwork worldwide.

03

WEB & DIGITAL

Swiss Punk principles translate surprisingly well to digital design. Broken grids, bold typography, and expressive layouts define cutting-edge web experiences.

WEB

Brutalist Websites

2010s Web Design Trend

Raw HTML aesthetics and intentionally "ugly" design echo Swiss Punk's anti-polish stance.

APP

Creative Agency Sites

Modern Portfolio Design

Design studios use Swiss Punk elements to showcase creativity and break from corporate templates.

UI

Experimental UI

Contemporary App Design

Bold borders, asymmetric layouts, and unexpected interactions bring Swiss Punk to mobile.

04

FASHION & BRANDING

From streetwear to high fashion, Swiss Punk's graphic intensity continues to influence visual identity and brand expression.

STYLE

Streetwear Graphics

Contemporary Fashion

Diagonal stripes, bold type, and graphic patterns define modern street fashion branding.

BRAND

Identity Systems

Brand Design

Companies seeking to appear innovative adopt Swiss Punk elements in their visual identities.

START BREAKING RULES

Now that you've seen Swiss Punk in action, it's time to apply these principles to your own work. Remember: understand the rules before you break them.
