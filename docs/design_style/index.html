What is Swiss Punk?

Swiss Punk (also known as New Wave Typography) emerged in the 1970s as a rebellious response to the rigid, minimalist Swiss typography that dominated corporate design.

Led by Wolfgang Weingart at the Basel School of Design, this movement embraced chaos, intuition, and expression—shattering the "less is more" philosophy with layered compositions, diagonal type, and intentionally broken grids.

KEY CHARACTERISTICS

Typography

  • Mixed typefaces & weights
  • Diagonal and rotated text
  • Inconsistent letter spacing
  • Bold stair-stepped layouts

Layout

  • Breaking the grid system
  • Asymmetric compositions
  • Layered collage elements
  • Chaotic but intentional

Philosophy

  • Intuition = Analytical skill
  • Expression over perfection
  • Anti-corporate aesthetic
  • Push limits of legibility
